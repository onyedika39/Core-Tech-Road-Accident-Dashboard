# Road Accident Analysis Dashboard 

## Overview
This project analyzes road accident casualties across 2021–2022 with a focus on severity, vehicle group, road type, road surface condition, urban vs rural patterns, and light conditions.  
The goal is to deliver a clear, decision-ready Excel dashboard that helps stakeholders quickly identify high-volume areas for safety intervention.

---

## Key Results (High-Level)
- **Total casualties analyzed:** 417,882  
- **Severity distribution:**
  - Fatal: 3,953  
  - Serious: 40,740  
  - Slight: 263,279  
- **Vehicle contribution:**
  - Cars: 78% (325,921)  
  - Other vehicles: 22% (91,961)  
- **Context patterns:**
  - Urban: 61.2% (255,900)  
  - Rural: 38.8% (162,000)  
  - Daylight: 73% (305,000)  
  - Dark: 27% (112,900)

## Insights
- Slight injuries account for the majority of overall casualties.
- Car-related incidents drive the largest share of casualties, representing the biggest opportunity for volume reduction.
- Single carriageways contribute the highest casualty volume among road types.
- High casualty counts in dry/daylight conditions likely reflect exposure volume.
- Urban areas show higher casualty volumes than rural areas.

---

## Recommendations
- Prioritize interventions focused on:
  - reducing car-related risks through targeted safety campaigns and enforcement.
  - engineering and monitoring high-volume single carriageway segments.
  - addressing wet/seasonal hazards with proactive public messaging and road maintenance.
  - strengthening night-time safety strategies in urban hotspots.
